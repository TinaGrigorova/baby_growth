FROM BIRTH TO THREE MONTHS

From birth to one month

At this stage baby should be 13 to 23 pounds if it’s a girl and 14 to 25 pounds if it’s a boy. They are changing every day that passes. Overall, the first mount the baby will adapt to the new world as well as the mommy. Between the sleepless night and the enjoy of the newborn the support could be a great opportunity, if possible, of course. Baby could have jaundice, which is normal, even if the index is high, it could be treated with blue light treatment for about 24hrs. You can always follow and check the following feeding chart:

Newborn 1 to 2 ounces 8 to 12 feedings
2 weeks 2 to 3 ounces 8 to 12 feedings
1 month 3 to 4 ounces 8 to 10 feedings
2 months 14 to 5 ounces 6 to 8 feedings
3 months 4 to 5 ounces 6 to 8 feedings

Baby's appetite will increase, and baby should gain between 0.5kg to 1kg and 2-2.5cm per month. Vitamin D is important for baby's development, that’s why baby needs one drop per day. Baby should start to discover his/her hands and feet soon. Baby’s head should start to be more controlled. When turned on his/her tummy the head should be more stable, the muscles are getting stronger, preparing for more advanced motor skills. Baby may have flaky scalp by this time, but this is nothing to worry about, it is normal and there are a lot of different products for treatment. Baby’s acne should start to fade. It helps make sure you’re both healthy and any issues are picked up early. You can ask any questions and to talk about how you’re feeling. It’s still early days so don’t expect to be in a regular routine. Don’t force it, in coming weeks and months it will happen. When your baby’s around 6 weeks old, you’ll be asked to see your GP. All mums and babies have this 6-week check. So much takes place during your baby’s first 2 months. What they can do and when will depend on lots of things, such as their: genetics, personality, health. day-to-day life with you. You’ll be given a personal child health record called the Red Book. You can use it to record information about your baby’s growth, development, tests and immunisations.

From two months to three months

Baby's hearing is getting better, as well as memory, touch feelings and sight. The little one facial expression will become more animated and varied. Smiles and giggles are just around the corner. Baby can now turn head and sight to the direction of the sound. Baby might not yet speak but they are actively observing and learning sounds and patterns of language. Baby's hand control is becoming more refined and soon baby will start grabbing objects, baby can also bring hands together.

FROM THREE MOUNTS TO SIX MONTS

From three to four months

By this time, most babies double their weight. From thiny arms and legs they are now sweet and fluffy, and they are getting stronger day by day. As for the weight and length all babies are different and as long as they keep growing as expected and at a steady rate, don’t worry about comparing them to other babies. The vocal abilities continue to develop, and baby should start to experiment more with sounds and soon laugh. It’s important to encourage baby to communicate and play it will stimulate their language skills and help them with further communication. Gentle gum massages are important and helpful for the baby's and the future teeth.

From four months to five months

More control over movements - baby's getting stronger and soon will be able to hold their head up unsupported. Babies love familiar faces, so soon expect a smile when baby sees someone they know. Soon baby will start to establish sleeping cycle, so it’s important to make a schedule for the sleep and eat for baby. // and about mommy: you'll hopefully start to feel more like yourself day by day// Baby should continue gain about 0.5kg per month and grow about 2cm. On average baby should be about 63.5cm to 68.5cm if boy and 61 to 66cm if girl.

From five months to six months

If your baby hasn’t started to roll over, they will definitely start this month. Baby should start to sleep through the whole night by now, of not - soon. Their teeth will start growing up as well, on which cases most babies are not taking it very well, they have temperature, sleepless nights but there are some medications that can help the babies get through with it. First their bottom central incisort will start grow, after that the upper ones and so on. Follow the chart below for more information.
In Conclusion: bottom incisors (bottom front teeth) – these are usually the first to come through, usually at around 5 to 7 months top incisors (top front teeth) – these tend to come through at about 6 to 8 months top lateral incisors (either side of the top front teeth) – these come through at around 9 to 11 months bottom lateral incisors (either side of the bottom front teeth) – these come through at around 10 to 12 months first molars (back teeth) – these come through at around 12 to 16 months canines (between the lateral incisors and the first molars) – these come through at around 16 to 20 months second molars – these come through at around 20 to 30 months Most children will have all of their milk teeth by the time they are between 2 and 3 years old.

WEANING - WHEN AND WHAT?

When is my baby ready?

  1. Grabing and putting toys in his/hers mouth
  2. Theething
  3. Adults food becomes very interesting
  4. Stay in sitting position and hold their head steady
  5. Swallow food - instead of spitting it
  6. They are curious about what you are eating
